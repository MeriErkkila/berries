Sustainability in berries

The final project of this year was to conduct a small research about the growth of berries in Finland. The data we received included the types of berries, the forests they grew in, the date of observation and the location of the berries. The objective was to discover any meaningful correlations in the data and relate the findings to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

The research produced significant results that could inform agricultural practices in the country. We discovered that the growth of berries in Finland is not affected by the population or the distance of the closest city, indicating that urbanization has not negatively impacted berry growth in the country. This finding is particularly significant for SDG 11, which aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. By showing that urbanization has not adversely affected berry growth, the study provides evidence that cities and their surrounding areas can coexist in a sustainable manner.

In addition, we also discovered a weak positive correlation between the growth of berries and the distance from the closest airport. However, this correlation coefficient was still considered negligible and does not significantly impact the growth of the berries.

The research also revealed that the optimal altitude for berry growth in Finland is around 150 meters, and while cloudberries are a minority in most altitudes, they are particularly well-suited to high altitudes. This finding could have significant implications for sustainable agriculture practices, particularly for SDG 2, which aims to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. By growing crops at the right altitude, farmers can reduce their dependence on artificial fertilizers and pesticides, leading to healthier soil and more sustainable farming practices.

Moreover, we also noted the potential impact of their findings on SDG 15, which seeks to protect, restore, and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation, and halt biodiversity loss. Berries are an important part of Finland's ecosystem, and their growth and sustainability are essential to maintaining the country's rich biodiversity. Although we haven't done extensive research on this topic, we believe it would be worth to further investigate this.

In conclusion, the research has important implications for sustainable agriculture practices and the SDGs. The findings demonstrate that urbanization has not negatively impacted berry growth in Finland, and that growing berries at the right altitude can lead to more sustainable farming practices. By providing evidence-based insights into the growth of berries in Finland, the study contributes to global efforts to create a more sustainable world. The team's research underscores the importance of conducting research on local environmental issues and shows how local findings can inform sustainable practices and contribute to the SDGs.
